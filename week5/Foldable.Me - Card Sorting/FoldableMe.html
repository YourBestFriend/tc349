Overview

This card sorting survey was conducted using 4 participants including myself.
It turned out that every participant ended up using three groups and this is
probably due to two reasons. First, I mentioned that I used three groups while
explaining the survey. And Second, there wasn't very much content on the site
and, therefore, there weren't very many cards to support a larger number of
groups.

I did not include the blog as a card, but rather the contents of the
blog as various cards. This was to see if anyone would create a "Blog" category
of their own for the content. One person did do this, though they included
other material and called it a "Stream".

There were certainly similarities between the sorts, mostly in the function of
each of the three groups. All but one of the participants had a main page, an
ordering section, and a "More Infomation" or "About Our Company" section. The
fourth participant opted for a "Stream" which held much of the same information.

The differences that occured seemed to me to be largely due to the participants
misunderstanding my labels to some extent. For example, they thought "Product
Reviews" were peer to peer, rather than from magazines. I believe this caused
a larger percentage of participants to place the reviews on the purchasing page,
much like Amazon or Ebay.

My sort in particular was fairly similiar to the Foldable.Me website, probably
because it's harder to see something through clear eyes after you've experienced
it one way. Like watching a movie then reading the book. There were a few
differences, however, and they included the following. I moved the website
launch article from the Blog section to the main page and transfered most of the
other blog posts into an "About Us" section.

I honestly wasn't exactly sure what was expected for this assignment, so
hopefully this is similar to what was intended by the project. I rethought
things a little after the survey was conducted oh ways I could have perhaps
made the cards a little clearer. However, at the risk of re-annoying my
friends I decided against another round of sorting.
