Panic.com Critique

Panic.com certainly offers users a squeaky clean interface... maybe too clean?
I suppose not. If the home page was anything other than a fork in the road for its
users the layout could become unintuitive. However, as it is used as a bulletin-board
showing shiny images of places you should want to visit, it probably serves its purpose.
Luckily, the links pop up description boxes when you mouse over them, otherwise the
web-page would seem like little more than an advertisement in a magazine.

I do like the gently shaded background, and the style of the icons and color scheme do
match the youthy innocence of the companies self-description. But there's just something
entirely too chipper about the whole thing... like the thin, bleached smiles on late-night
infomercials. Nobody's that nice...
